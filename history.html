History of the Parti Yorkie breed

    

The Parti Yorkie is not a regular Yorkie. They have a very rare colorway of black, white and tan. It is the special genetic DNA which makes them unique. The Parti Yorkie gene is a recessive trait, so the mother and the father must carry the gene to produce a Parti Yorkie puppy. Parti Yorkies are not mutts and are healthy breeds. Back in the 1800's the Parti Yorkie was seen as odd, so they were usually put down. It wasn't until the 1980's when they were displayed in dog shows, were they seen as luxurious. After AKC (the American Kennel Club) officially acknowledged the Parti Yorkie in the year 2000, they have been highly sought after and there are not enough on the market to satisfy the demand for them.
