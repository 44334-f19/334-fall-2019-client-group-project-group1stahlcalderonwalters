History of the Party Yorkie breed

    

The Party Yorkie is not a regular Yorkie. They have a very rare colorway, and they have a special genetic DNA which makes them unique. The Party Yorkie gene is a recessive trait, so the mother and the father must carry the gene to produce a Party Yorkie puppy. Party Yorkies are not mutts and are healthy breeds. Back in the 1800's the Party Yorkie was seens as odd, so they were usually put down. It wasn't until the 1980's when they were displayed in dog shows, were they seen as luxurious. After AKC (the American Kennel Club) officialy acknoledged the Party Yorkie in the year 2000, they have been highly sought after and there are not enough on the market to satisfy the demand for them.
